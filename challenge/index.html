About

The main goal of the challenge is to evaluate and benchmark new and better methods for GQA-LT and VG8K-LT benchmarks proposed in Exploring Long Tail Visual Relationship Recognition with Large Vocabulary. This is a new baseline proposed on top of VG and GQA datasets, but specifically focussed on long-tail recognition. For GQA-LT ( 1,703 object classes and 310 relation classes), the most frequent object and relationship categories have 374,282 and 1,692,068 examples, and the least frequent have 1 and 2 examples, respectively. This results in factors of around 300,000+ for objects and around 1.7 million for relations between the most frequent and least frequent classes. For VG8K-LT (5,330 objects classes and 2000 relation classes), the most frequent object and relationship categories have 196,944 and 618,687 examples, and the least frequent have 14 and 18 examples, respectively, which leads to factors of approximately 14,000 for objects and 34,000 for relations.

Task

The main task for both of these benchmarks is to predict relationship and at the same time subject/object associated with the triplet. During training and evaluation, the ground-truth bounding boxes are already provided and hence the main aim is for the classification of sbj/obj and the rel associated with them. Further details about the metrics used for evaluation and also the submission site for both the benchmarks can be found here. Also download the train/val datasets from here, along with the starter code.
